How crypto-to-card payments work

Crypto credit cards don’t send blockchain transactions at the checkout. Instead, they convert assets off-chain or pre-load fiat balance that rides Visa/Mastercard rails.

  • • Select asset in wallet (BTC, ETH, USDT…)
  • • Conversion happens on issuer side
  • • Authorization uses a fiat amount
  • • Card networks never see your crypto
  • • Cashbacks may be paid in tokens

Types of crypto-linked cards

Prepaid crypto card

Convert before you spend. Functions like a digital gift balance.

Auto-convert card

Real-time conversion at checkout. Spend directly from your crypto wallet.

Stablecoin card

Load USDC/USDT and spend in local fiat currency instantly.

CBDC-compatible cards

Future-facing cards that may support tokenized bank money.

Why people choose crypto cards

  • • Global usability (works anywhere Visa/Mastercard does)
  • • Spend stablecoins without cashing out manually
  • • Earn rewards in crypto
  • • Ability to segment spending via tokens
  • • Lower fees than some exchange withdrawals

Tokens, wallets & virtual cards

Crypto cards still rely on modern card tokenization:

  • • Network tokens for safer authorizations
  • • Device tokens (Apple Pay / Google Pay)
  • • Virtual numbers for subscriptions
  • • On-chain tokens for loyalty payouts
